Here are some of the things I have done.

Whether it be building a mechanized beaver or completely taking apart a computer, my first year at Olin has involved many projects.

IT Technician

I work at Olin's IT Help Desk, maintaining the infrastructure of the college. This involves both diagnosing software and hardware issues, replacing faulty components, and working with users to figure out problems.

Modeling the Real World

When I started going to an engineering school, I didn't expect to be using medical knowledge for a Modeling and Simulation project. However, one of the first projects involved modeling the rate of dialysis using real world data.

Building a Beaver

When I think of a kids toy, a beaver is not the first thing that comes to mind. However, for Design Nature, my group built and tested a mechanized swimming beaver that could be controlled remotely. It ultimately was well received by a group of grade school students.

Computer Science

A recent Software Design project called for using both recursion and randomly generated math equations to form computer generated artwork.

Line Following Robot

Modeling and Control was the standard introduction to circuits course, with a final project. I worked with one other student to build a line following robot using photodiodes and a circuit which would compare between white and black and follow a course.

Creativity among Engineers

I also keep practicing piano, something that has been a part of my life for 14 years now. This year, I was able to take The Wired Ensemble, where I composed two new pieces bi-weekly for an audience for a whole semester.
